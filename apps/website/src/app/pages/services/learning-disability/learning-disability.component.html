Learning Disability

Regardless of age or presumptive ability, we think that everyone can live a good life with favorable outcomes. Learning disability can however make a variety of daily activities and circumstances more difficult for affected persons and their families.

We put a lot of effort into getting to know people so that we can provide assistance that is as unique as they are. This includes learning how they prefer to be supported, what they hope to accomplish in life, and what matters to them.

Our specialist learning disability services
Each individual we support has an outcome-focused support plan that examines how we might collaborate to help them realize their goals. This could involve setting objectives for improving one's health, participating in the community, maintaining relationships with loved ones, and learning new skills.
We encourage and support people to complete household chores, go shopping, manage their finances, use neighbourhood amenities like recreation centres and libraries, and pursue their goals of furthering their education or finding employment.
